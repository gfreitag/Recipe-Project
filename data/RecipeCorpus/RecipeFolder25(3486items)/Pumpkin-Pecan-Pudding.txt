Title: Pumpkin Pecan Pudding
Categories: Diabetic, Dessert, Fruit
Yield: 12 sweet ones
Dry sugar substitute equal
-to 1/4 cup sugar
1/2 ts Salt;
1 ts Vanilla;
1/2 c  Pecans; chopped
16 oz (1 cn) solid pack pumpkin;
12 oz Evaporated skim milk;
1/3 c  Brown sugar;
2 lg Eggs;
1 ts To 2 ts pumpkin pie spice;
Place pumpkin, milk, (add enough water to equal 1-1/2 cups, if
necessary), brown sugar, eggs, pumpkin pie spice, dry sugar
substitute, salt and vanilla in mixer bowl and mix at medium speed
until smooth and creamy. Pour into an 8" square baking pan that has
been sprayed with pan spray or greased with margarine.  Sprinkle
pecans evenly over top of pudding. Bake at 325 degrees (300 if are
using a glass baking dish) for about 1 hour, or until a knife comes
out clean from the center of the pudding. Cool to room temperature.
Cut three by four. Yield: 12 servings Food Exchange per serving: 1
BREAD EXCHANGE + 1 FAT EXCHANGE; CAL: 1O5; CHO: 14g; PRO: 4g; FAT:
4g; CHO: 47mg; Low-cholesterol diets: Omit eggs. Use 1/2 cup egg
whites or liquid egg substitute. Low-sodium diets: Omit salt. Source:
Desserts for Diabetics by Mabel Cavaiani, R.D. Brought to you and
yours via Nancy O'Brion and her Meal-Master.
