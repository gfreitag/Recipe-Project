Title: Bajan Seasonin'
Categories: Salsa/sauce, Caribbean
Yield: 2 1/2 cups
1    Celery stalk
1    Scotch bonnet or
2    Jalapeno pepper
Seeded and minced
1 bn Flat-leaf parsley, stemmed
Coarsely chopped (abt 1 cup)
1 bn Thyme, stemmed or
1 tb Dried thyme
1 bn Marjoram, stemmed or
1 tb Dried marjoram (opt)
1/2 c  Lime juice (3-4 limes)
Salt and pepper
1    Whole head garlic
Broken into cloves
And peeled (10-12 cloves)
1 md Onion(s), halved
Coarsely chopped
3 bn Chives or
2 bn Scallions
Trimmed, coarsely chopped
1    Green bell pepper, cored
Seeded, coarsely chopped
1    Red bell pepper, cored
Seeded, coarsely chopped
Coarsely puree the garlic, onion, bell peppers, celery, chili peppers,
chives, herbs, and lime juice in a food processor. Correct the
seasoning, adding salt and pepper or lime juice to taste. The mixture
should be very flavorful.

Store in a glass jar with a nonreactive lid. Refrigerated, it will
keep for several weeks.

Makes enough for 2 pounds of meat, chicken, or seafood.

Marinating Time for Seasonin'

12-24 hours
:Whole Chicken
: (put some in the cavity, some under
:  the skin, and rub the rest over
:  the skin)

8-12 hours
:Whole Pork Shoulder or Tenderloin

4-6  hours
:Whole Fish
: (put some in the cavity, some in
:  slits in the flesh, and rub the
:  rest over the skin)

2-4  hours
:Chicken Breasts, Steaks, Pork Chops

1-2  hours
:Shrimp and Fish Fillets
