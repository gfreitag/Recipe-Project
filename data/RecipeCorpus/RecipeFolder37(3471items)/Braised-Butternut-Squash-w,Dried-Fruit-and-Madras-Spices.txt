Title: Braised Butternut Squash w/Dried Fruit and Madras Spices
Categories: Vegetables
Yield: 6 servings
(or more to taste)
1 1/2 lb Butternut squash
Or pumpkin, peeled
Seeded, cut in 1/2" chunks
1 ts Ground cinnamon
1 ts Ground cumin
1 ts Ground coriander
1 ts Whole mustard seed
2 ts Curry powder
2 c  Chicken stock
Salt and pepper
1/2 c  Cilantro leaves, minced
1/4 c  Dark or golden raisins
1/4 c  Dried apricots
Cut in 1/4" strips
1/2 c  Dry red wine
3 tb Olive oil
1 tb Ginger, minced
1 md Garlic clove(s), minced
1 lg Spanish onion
Coarsely chopped
1    Red bell pepper, stemmed
Seeded, cut in 1/4" dice
1    Serrano or jalapeno pepper
Stemmed, seeded, minced
1. Heat oven to 400 F. Soak raisins and apricots in wine; set aside.

2. Heat oil in medium skillet over medium heat. Add ginger and garlic;
saute until fragrant but not brown, about 1 min. Increase heat to
medium-high; add onion, peppers (sweet and hot), and squash. Saute
until squash starts to brown, 3-5 min. Add dried fruit, wine, and
spices; cook to blend flavors, about 1 min. Add stock, bring to
simmer.

3. Cover skillet and braise vegetables in oven until squash is tender
but not mushy, 10-12 min. Season to taste with salt and pepper;
garnish with cilantro.
