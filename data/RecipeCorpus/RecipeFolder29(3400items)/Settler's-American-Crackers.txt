Title: Settler's American Crackers
Categories: Breads, Crackers, Settler's
Yield: 1 servings
No Ingredients Found
One quart of flour, into which rub two ounces of butter; dissolve one
teaspoonful of salaratus (soda) in a wine-glass or cup of warm water;
half a teaspoonful of salt, and milk sufficient to mix it into a
stiff, smooth dough; beat it for half an hour, working it well with
the rolling pin; make into thin biscuit, or small round balls flatted
in the middle with the thumb,and bake till dry and crisp. Origin: The
Canadian Settler's Guide, written in 1855 Shared by: Sharon Stevens.
