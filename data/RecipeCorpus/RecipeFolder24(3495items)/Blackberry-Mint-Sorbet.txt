Title: Blackberry Mint Sorbet
Categories: Dessert, Fruit
Yield: 1 batch
1 c  Red Burgundy wine
Lemon juice (opt'l.)
Additional berries
-- for garnish
1 c  Sugar
1 c  ;Water
1 c  Fresh mint leaves
-- tightly packed
4 c  Blackberries or dewberries
In a medium saucepan, combine the sugar, water and mint.  Bring to a
boil, then simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally, for about
5 minutes to make a mint-flavored simple syrup.  Strain, discarding
mint leaves, and cool. In a food processor, coarsely puree the
berries. Turn into a bowl and stir in the wine and mint syrup. Taste
and add lemon juice if you prefer a tarter flavor.

Pour the liquid into an ice cream maker and freeze according to
manufacturer's directions until firm.  Garnish with additional
berries.

Yield: 1 1/2 quarts.

From "Cooking with Herbs" by Emelie Tolley and Chris Mead.  New York:
Clarkson N. Potter, Inc., 1989.  Pg. 69.  Posted by Cathy Harned.
