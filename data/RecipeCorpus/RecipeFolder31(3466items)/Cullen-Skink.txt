Title: Cullen Skink
Categories: British isl, Soup/stew, Fish
Yield: 6 servings
1 lb Smoked haddock, find it
-frozen in the fish store
1 qt Cold water
1 md Yellow onion, diced
3 c  Milk
2 1/4 c  Cooked mashed potato
1 tb Butter
Salt and pepper to t
Tiny pinch of ground
MMMMM--------------------------GARNISH-------------------------------
Butter
Chopped parsley
Place the fish in a 4-quart shallow pan along with 1 quart of water.
B to a boil and reduce heat. Simmer, partly covered, for 10 minutes.
Rem the cod, reserving the broth, and allow it to cool. Debone and
flake t fish. Return it to the pan. Add the onion, cover, and simmer
for 20 minutes.

In a small saucepan heat the milk to a boil and add to the broth and
flaked fish. Simmer 4 minutes, partly covered. Stir in the remaining
ingredients, except the garnish, and bring to serving temperature.
Sal and fresh ground pepper to taste. Top with the butter and parsley
and serve hot. Recipe from the Furgal Gourmet.  The term skink comes
from Gaelic and means "esence". Submitted By TANANA REYNOLDS   On
06-16-95
