Title: Sesame Coated Fish Sticks
Categories: Tvfn
Yield: 4 servings
1/2 c  Each white and black sesame
Seeds
1 c  Light or salt-reduced soy
Sauce
2 ts Hot pepper sesame oil
2 tb Lemon juice
2 1/2 lb Fresh halibut, cut into I
Inch thick sticks
Flour for dredging
4    Egg whites, beaten until
Frothy
3 tb Peanut or other vegetable
Oil
Sweet-Soy Dipping Sauce
(recipe follows)
Combine sesame seeds in a bowl and set aside. In a separate bowl
combine the soy sauce, hot pepper sesame oil and lemon juice.
Marinate fish sticks for 5-10 minutes in the soy mixture turning to
coat. Dredge in flour, shaking off excess. Next, coat fish stick with
egg whites and then roll in sesame seeds to coat evenly on all sides.
Heat an oven proof sauté pan over moderate heat and add the oil.
Sauté the fish sticks on all sides until the sesame seeds just begin
to brown. Finish cooking if necessary in a preheated 425 degree oven
for 4-6 minutes or until fish is just done and firm to the touch. Be
careful not to overcook. Serve immediately with Sweet-Soy Dipping
Sauce.

Yield: 6-8 servings
