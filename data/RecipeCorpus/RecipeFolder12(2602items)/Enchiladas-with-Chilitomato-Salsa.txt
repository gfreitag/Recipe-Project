Title: Enchiladas with Chilitomato Salsa
Categories: Toohot, Mexican
Yield: 4 Servings
6    Dried Ancho chilies
2 c  Water
1/2 c  White vinegar
2 tb Olive oil
4    Garlic cloves, sliced
1 tb Dried oregano
1/2 ts Ground coriander
1/4 ts Ground cumin
2    Tomatoes, peeled, seeded,
Diced
1 lg Red onion, diced
1 c  Vegetable oil
12 lg Corn tortillas
1 c  Shredded Manchego cheese
1 c  Shredded Panela cheese
1/2 c  Crumbled Cotija cheese
Chopped fresh cilantro
Toast chilies over gas flame, turning frequently.  Remove from heat.
Bring water and vinegar to boil in heavy medium saucepan.  Add
chilies. Remove from heat.  Let stand until softened, about 20
minutes. Heat oil in heavy large skillet over medium heat.  Add
garlic, oregano, coriander and cumin and stir until fragrant.  Add
tomatoes and cook until soft. Transfer to processor.  Add chilies,
soaking liquid and half of onion. Puree until smooth.  Season to
taste with salt and pepper.

Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Pour vegetable oil into large skillet
and heat over medium heat.  One at a time dip tortillas into sauce
and shake off excess.  Transfer tortillas, one at a time, to skillet
and cook 10 seconds on each side.  Drain on rack.

Combine cheeses in medium bowl.  Reserve 1/2 cup cheese.  Divide
remaining cheese into 12 portions.  Spoon onto lower half of each
tortilla. Sprinkle remaining onion over.  Roll up each tortilla to
form tube.

Arrange on baking sheet, seam side down.  Bake until cheese melts,
about 3 to 5 minutes.  Heat remaining sauce.  Spoon onto 4 plates.
Top with 3 enchiladas.  Sprinkle with remaining cheese and cilantro
and serve.
