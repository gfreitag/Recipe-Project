Title: Jerk Chicken Wings
Categories: Poultry, Appetizers, Salsa/sauce
Yield: 8 servings  JERK CHICKEN WINGS (Makes 48) ****************** Don't worry, be
happy! The name of this recipe is no reflection on the cook; jerk is
a fiery Jamaican marinade for chicken, pork, or beef.

24 whole chicken wings (about 4 pounds) 8 scallions, cut into 1-inch
pieces 4 fresh jalapeno peppers, seeded and coarsely chopped 2 T
distilled white vinegar 1 T ground allspice 4 garlic cloves, chopped
2 t dried thyme 1 t salt 1/2 t freshly ground pepper 1/4 t cayenne
1/4 c vegetable oil Lime wedges
> >> 1. Rinse
chicken with cold water and pat dry. Cut off and discard pointed tip
of each wing and halve wings at the main joint.

2. In a food processor, combine scallions, jalapeno peppers, vinegar,
allspice, garlic, thyme, salt, pepper, and cayenne. Process until well
blended. With machine on, slowly pour in oil and puree until a thick
paste forms.

3. In a large bowl, combine chicken wings and jerk paste. Toss until
wings are well coated. Cover and refrigerate overnight

4. Preheat broiler. Arrange wings on broiler pan about 6 inches from
heat and broil, turning once, until nicely browned outside and cooked
through, about 20 minutes total. Serve warm or at room temperature
with lime wedges and lots of napkins.
