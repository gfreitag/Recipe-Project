Title: FALL VEGETABLE RAGOUT - Country Living
Categories: Country liv, Main dish, Vegetables, Soup/stew
Yield: 5 servings (
1    Bay leaf
1 cn (14 1/2-oz) vegetable broth
1/2 lb Green beans
1/4 lb Wax beans
8 sm Leaves Swiss chard or kale
3 sm Parsnips
1 lg Ripe red tomato
2 tb All-purpose flour
Salt and ground black pepper
2 tb Butter or margarine
1 tb Olive oil
1 lg Onion, chopped
1/2 lb Mushrooms, halved
4 md Carrots, peeled and cut into
-1 1/2-inch lengths
1    Clove garlic, finely chopped
1 tb Fresh thyme leaves OR
1 ts Dried thyme leaves
1. In 5-quart Dutch oven, heat butter and oil over medium-high heat.
Add onion and saute for 1 minute. Add mushrooms and saute for 5
minutes. Stir in carrots, garlic, thyme, bay leaf and 1 C broth.
Cover and heat to boiling. Reduce the heat to low and cook vegetables
10 minutes or until carrots are almost tender.

2. Meanwhile, trim and cut green and wax beans into 1-inch lengths. If
using Swiss chard, cut stems into 1-inch-wide lengths and cut the
leaves into 1/2-inchwide ribbons. If using kale, discard stems and
cut leaves into 1/4-inch-wide ribbons. Peel and slice the parsnips.
Core and dice the tomato.

3. Add the beans, chard stems, and parsnips to the vegetables in the
Dutch oven and stir until well mixed. Cover; increase heat to high
and heat to boiling. Reduce the heat to low and cook until all the
veg- etables are tender. Add chard or kale leaves and tomatoes to the
vegetables and heat through.

4. In a cup, combine remaining 3/4 C broth with the flour until
smooth. Stir into the vegetable ragout and season with salt and
pepper to taste. Cook, stirring, until bubbly and thickened. Spoon
ragout into serving bowls.
