Title: Reuben Soup
Categories: Soup/stew, Vegetable, Homemade
Yield: 1 servings
1/2 t  Baking soda
2 T  Cornstarch
2 T  Water
3/4 c  Sauerkraut, rinsed
-drained
2 c  Light cream
3/4 c  Sauerkraut, rinsed and
-drained
2 c  Chopped cooked corned beef
1 c  Rye croutons, optional
4 oz Shredded Swiss cheese
Salt and pepper to taste
"When we're lucky, this
-soup is served in the staff
Cafeteria at school.  The
-cooks have served it for
-years
And it remains a special
-favorite." - Mary Lindell
1/2 c  Chopped onion
1/2 c  Sliced celery
2 T  Butter or margarine
1 c  Chicken broth
1 c  Beef broth
In a large saucepan, saute onion and celery in butter until tender.
Add broth and baking soda.  Combine cornstarch and water; add to pan.
Bring to a boil; boil for 2 minutes, stirring occasionally. Reduce
heat. Add sauerkraut, cream and corned beef; simmer for 15 minutes.
Add cheese; heat until melted.  Add salt and pepper.  Garnish with
croutons, if desired. Serves:  6 (1 1/2 quarts) From: "Taste of Home"
Magazine, October/November 1994 Posted by: Debbie Carlson - Cooking
