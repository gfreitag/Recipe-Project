Title: Ginger-Flavored Chinese-Style Adzuke Beans
Categories: Beans, Side dish, Chinese, Herbs/spice
Yield: 4 servings
Star anise; optional
1 ts Salt
1    Onion
1/4 c  Molasses; or plum jelly
2 tb Sweet sherry
1 tb Soy sauce; (Tamari)
2 c  Adzuke beans, dried
1 cl Garlic
6    Peppercorns
Ginger, fresh; 1 inch piece
Cinnamon stick; 1 inch piece
10    Coriander seeds
2    Cloves; whole
Put half of the adzuke beans into a pot with 3 cups of water, and
bring them to a boil.  Tie the garlic, peppercorns, ginger, cinnamon,
coriander, cloves and a small piece of the star anise, if using, into
a spice bag. Put the bag into the bean pot with the salt, remaining
adzuke beans and the onion.  Bring the mixture to a boil again, then
cover and cook for about two hours, or until the beans are tender.
Remove the onion and the spice bag.  Stir in the molasses or jelly,
sherry and tamari soy sauce. misc01

Source: Susan Restino, Mrs. Restino's Country Kitchen
