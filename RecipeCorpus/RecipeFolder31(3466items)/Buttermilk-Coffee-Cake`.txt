Title: Buttermilk Coffee Cake`
Categories: Breakfast
Yield: 12 servings
2 1/4 c  All-Purpose Flour
1 c  Packed Light Brown Sugar --
-rub thru' a sieve*
3/4 c  Granulated Sugar
3/4 c  Vegetable Oil, Preferably
Corn Oil
2 ts Ground Cinnamon
1/4 ts Ground Ginger
1/4 ts Salt
1 c  Chopped Walnuts
1 c  Buttermilk
1 lg Egg -- beaten
1 ts Baking Powder
1 ts Baking Soda, Sifted To
-Remove Lumps
* Rub brown sugar through a sieve to remove lumps, only if necessary.
1. Preheat oven to 350 F. Butter and flour a 12 by 8-inch baking pan,
shaking out the excess flour. 2. In a medium bowl, whisk the flour,
brown and granulated sugars, oil, 1 tsp of the cinnamon, ginger, and
the salt until smooth. Transfer 3/4 cup of the mixture to a small
bowl. Add the walnuts to the small bowl and mix until well combined.
Set the nut mixture aside to be used later as the topping. 3. Add the
buttermilk, remaining 1 tsp cinnamon, egg, baking powder, and baking
soda to the remaining batter in the large bowl and whisk just until
smooth. Transfer to the baking pan. Sprinkle the nut mixture evenly
over the top. 4. Bake until the topping is browned and a toothpick
inserted in the center of the pan comes out clean, about 30 mins.
Cool the cake in the pan for at least 20 mins. Serve the cake warm or
at room temperature. Makes 12 servings from Clyde Nelson of The Home
Ranch of Clark, CO.
