Title: Smoked and Fresh Salmon Spread - Country Living
Categories: Fish, Appetizers
Yield: 3 cups
2 tb Unsalted butter
1/2 c  Sliced shallots or chopped
-onion
1 md Carrot, peeled and
-thinly sliced
3/4 lb Red salmon steak, cut
-1 inch thick
1 tb Cognac or brandy
1/2 lb Sliced smoked salmon, cut up
1/2    8-oz package cream cheese,
-softened and cut into small
0chunks
1 ts Finely grated lemon rind
1 tb Lemon juice
1 ts Dijon-style prepared
-mustard
1/8 ts Ground white pepper
1 tb Red salmon caviar (opt.)
Fresh dill sprigs (opt.)
Assorted vegetables (large
-carrot slices, Belgian
0endive leaves, cucumber
-slices, sweet red- pepper
-strips), crackers, small
-slices dark bread (opt.)
1. In large skillet, melt butter over low heat. Add shallots and
carrot; cover and cook 5 minutes, stirting once. Meanwhile, rinse
salmon steak under cold water and cut lengthwise in half. Place
salmon steak halves over vegetables in skillet and drizzle with
cognac. Cover and cook 10 minutes, turning once, or until fish is
firm and flakes easily. Remove from heat; uncover and cool to room
temperature.

2. Remove skin and bones from cooked salmon. Place salmon in food
processor fitted with chopping blade. Add shallots, carrot, and
skillet juices to salmon. Process until smooth. Add smoked salmon,
cream cheese, lemon rind, lemon juice, mustard, and pepper. Process
just until salmon is finely chopped but not completely smooth.

3. Mound salmon mixture onto small serving plate; cover with plastic
wrap and refrigerate 1 hour before serving.

4. Just before serving, garnish salmon with caviar spooned over the
top and place dill sprigs on the plate, if desired. Serve with
vegetables, crackers, or bread, if desired.
