Title: PORK STEW WITH CORNMEAL DUMPLINGS
Categories: Soup/stew, Main dish, Crockpot
Yield: 1 batch  1 lb boneless pork shoulder roast 1 clove garlic, minced 1 TB cooking
oil 4 md carrots, cut into 1/2-inch pieces (2 cups) 2 md potatoes,
peeled and cubed (2 cups) 12 oz can (1 1/2 cups) beer 1/4 c quick
cooking tapioca 1 TB sugar 1 TB Worcestershire sauce 2 bay leaves 1
ts dried thyme, crushed 1/2 ts salt 1/4 ts ground nutmeg 1/4 ts
pepper 28 oz can tomatoes, cut up Cornmeal dumplings (recipe follows)
2 TB shredded cheddar cheese

Cut pork into 1-inch cubes.  In a large skillet brown pork and garlic
in hot oil.  Drain well

Meanwhile, in a 3 1/2 or 4 quart crockery cooker combine carrots,
potatoes, beer, tapioca, sugar, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaves,
thyme, salt, nutmeg and pepper.  Stir in browned meat and undrained
tomatoes. Cover; cook on low heat setting for 9-11 hours or on high
heat setting for 4-5 hours.

If stew was cooked on low heat setting, turn crockery cooker to high
heat setting.  Prepare Cornmeal Dumplings.  Remove bay leaves. Stir
stew; drop dumplings by tablespoonfuls onto stew. Cover; cook for 50
minutes more (do not lift cover).  Sprinkle dumplings with cheese.
Makes 4 servings.

For 5 or 6 quart crockery cooker:  Use one 1 1/2 pound boneless pork
shoulder roast, 5 medium carrots, and 3 medium potatoes. Leave
remaining ingredient amounts the same.  Prepare as above. Makes 6
servings.

For 1 quart crockery cooker:  Use 1 medium carrot, halve remaining
stew ingredients, and omit Cornmeal Dumplings and cheese. Prepare as
above. Cook for 10-12 hours.  Serve stew with corn muffins or corn
bread. Makes 2 servings.

CORNMEAL DUMPLINGS

1/2 c all-purpose flour 1/2 c shredded cheddar cheese 1/3 c yellow
cornmeal 1 ts baking powder ds pepper 1 beaten egg 2 TB milk 2 TB
cooking oil

In a medium mixing bowl stir together flour, cheddar cheese, cornmeal,
baking powder, and pepper.

Combine beaten egg, milk, and oil.  Add to flour mixture; stir with a
fork just till combined.  Use in Pork Stew with Cornmeal Dumplings,
above.
