Title: Turkey Spaghetti Bake
Categories: Turkey, Pasta, Main dish, Vegetable, Cheese/eggs
Yield: 6 - 8 folks
MMMMM-----------------------SPAGHETTI BAKE----------------------------
1 tb Margarine;
1 c  Ham;*(optional) diced cooked
1/2 lb Mushrooms, thinly sliced
3 c  Turkey;* cooked diced
Cream sauce
2 tb Parsley; fresh minced
2 tb Chives; fresh minced -=OR=-
2 tb Green onions; minced
1 pk (8 oz) spaghetti, cooked
-until barely tender
1/2 c  Parmesan -=OR=-
1/2 c  Swiss cheese;
3 tb Dry sherry -=OR=-
3 tb Dry vermouth;
Salt; to taste
Fresh ground pepper; taste
1 tb Butter -=OR=-
MMMMM------------------------CREAM SAUCE-----------------------------
1/2 ts Paprika;
1/2 ts Salt;
1/4 ts Ground pepper; freshly
2    Egg yolks; sightly beaten
3 tb Butter -=OR=-
3 tb Margarine;
3 tb All-purpose flour;
2 1/2 c  Half & half -=OR=-
2 1/2 c  Milk; (low-fat)
*If ham is not used, increase turkey by 1 cup.

TURKEY SPAGHETTI BAKE:  Drain cooked spaghetti.  Toss with 1/4 cheese
and 1 tb sherry or vermouth; spread in greased shallow 1-1/2 quart
baking dish and sprinkle lightly salt and pepper.  Heat butter or
margarine in skillet saute' ham and mushrooms for 5 minutes, stirring
frequently. Place in large bowl with turkey and toss.  Stir into
Cream Sauce and heat well; stir into Cream Sauce and heat well; stir
in remaining wine, parsley, and chives or green onion.  Correct
seasoning if necessary. Spoon mixture carefully over spaghetti.
Sprinkle top with remain 1/4 cheese. Bake in preheated oven 350
degrees for 25 to 30 minutes. CREAM SAUCE: Melt butter or margarine
in medium saucepan; add flour and cook, stirring for 3 minutes.  Add
half and half or milk and stir over medium heat until sauce has
thickened about 10 minutes.  Add paprika, salt and pepper.  Stir 1/2
cup hot sauce into yolks; stir quickly back into remaining hot sauce.
Cook, stirring, for 3 minutes. Makes about 3 cups.  COMMENT: Good for
an after-the-holiday supper Source: Miram B. Loo's "Never Say
Leftovers". Brought to you and yours via Nancy O'Brion and her
Meal-Master. Submitted By NANCY O'BRION   On   12-28-94
