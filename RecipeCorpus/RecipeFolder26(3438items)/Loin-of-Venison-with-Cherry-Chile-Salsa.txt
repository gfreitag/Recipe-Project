Title: Loin of Venison with Cherry Chile Salsa
Categories: Game, Main dish, Fruit, Chilies, Salsa/sauce
Yield: 4 to 6 servi
1    Pear, finely chopped
-(unpeeled)
1/2 c  Peeled, diced jicama
1    Venison loin (or 2 lb
-backstrap)
1/2 c  Apple Cider
2 c  Dried cherries
1/2 - 1 c  Cleaned, roasted green chiles

Instructions:

1.  Preheat oven to 400F.  Rinse and dry the venison.  Tie with a
string so that it is of uniform thickness.  Lightly season woth salt
and pepper. Heat a saute pan with a thin coating of olive oil.

2.  When the oil is hot, brown the venison on all sodes, then place
the venison in the oven for 15 to 20 minutes, until medium rare.
Allow the venison to sit at room temperature for at least 20 minutes
before slicing thinly to serve.

3.  Place apple cider in a small saucepan over moderate heat.  Add
dried cherries and remove from heat when liquid is hot, not boiling.
Cover pan with lid and set aside, off heat, until cherries are
plumped and soft, about 20 minutes.

4.  Add chopped pear, jicama and chilies to cherries.  There should be
little or no liquid left in pan; drain if necessary.

Source/Author: Kelowna Daily Courier / typo Bob Shiells
