Title: Wet Jerk Seasonings
Categories: Salsa/sauce, Caribbean
Yield: 1 1/2 cups
3 tb Salt or to taste
2 ts Thyme, chopped
Or dried thyme
2 ts Ground allspice
1 ts Black pepper
1/2 ts Grated nutmeg
1/4 ts Ground cinnamon
1/8 ts Ground cloves
1/4 c  Lime juice
Or white vinegar
3 tb Soy sauce
2 tb Vegetable oil
1 tb Brown sugar
2 tb Water or as needed
2    (to 12) Scotch bonnet
Chilies, stemmed
1 md Onion(s), coarsely chopped
4    (to 5) shallots,
Coarsely chopped
(abt 1/4 cup)
2 bn Chives or scallions
Trimmed to remove roots
Coarsely chopped
1 c  Flat-leaf parsley
Coarsely chopped
4    Garlic clove(s)
Coarsely chopped
1 tb Ginger, finely chopped
Combine the chilies, onion, shallots, chives, parsley, and garlic in
a food processor and puree to a coarse paste. Work in the ginger,
salt, thyme, allspice, black pepper, nutmeg, cinnamon, cloves, lime
juice, soy sauce, vegetable oil, and brown sugar. Correct the
seasoning, adding soy sauce or salt to taste as needed. The mixture
should be very flavorful. Add water as needed to make a thick but
pourable paste.

Makes enough for 2 pounds meat, chicken, or seafood.

Marinating Time for Jerk

12-24 hours
:Whole Chicken
: (put some of the seasoning in the
:  cavity, some under the skin, and
:  rub the rest over the skin)

12-24 hours
:Whole Pork Shoulder or Tenderloins
: (make tiny holes in the meat with
:  the tip of a knife and rub a little
:  of the seasoning in each hole)

12-24 hours
:Rack of Lamb and Beef Tenderloins
: (make tiny holes in the meat with
:  the tip of a knife and rub a little
:   of the seasoning in each hole)

4-6  hours
:Whole Fish
: (put some of the seasoning in the
:  cavity, some in slits in the flesh
:  and rub the rest over the skin)

2-4  hours
:Chicken Breasts, Steaks, Pork Chops

1/2-1  hour
:Shrimp and Fish Fillets
