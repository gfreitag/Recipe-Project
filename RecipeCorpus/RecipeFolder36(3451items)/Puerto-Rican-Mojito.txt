Title: Puerto Rican Mojito
Categories: Salsa/sauce, Caribbean
Yield: 2 cups
1 1/2 tb Capers, drained
1 1/2 ts Dried oregano
1/2 c  Cilantro leaves
Washed and stemmed
1/4 c  Tomato paste
2 tb Extra-virgin olive oil
1 tb Lime juice
Salt and pepper to taste
6    Aji dulce peppers or
1 1/2 tb Red bell pepper, diced
1/2    Green bell pepper, diced
5    Garlic clove(s)
Coarsely chopped
2    Shallots, coarsely chopped
1    Tomato
Peeled and seeded
Traditionally served as a dipping sauce for plantain chips and fried
mashed green plantains. It's also great for dipping tortilla chips
and makes a fine cocktail sauce for shrimp and other seafood.

Combine the peppers, garlic, shallots, tomato, capers, oregano, and
cilantro in a food processor and grind to a smooth puree. Work in the
tomato paste, olive oil, lime juice, and salt and pepper.

Transfer to a clean jar with a nonreactive lid. Refrigerated, it will
keep for 1 week.
