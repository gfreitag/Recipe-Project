Title: Salsa de Aji (Ecuadorian Hot Salsa)
Categories: Salsa/sauce, Vegetables, So. america
Yield: 1 +cups
2 lg Tomatoes; seeds removed,
-finely chopped
1 md Onion; finely chopped
1 tb Fresh cilantro; finely
-chopped
4 lg Aji chiles; seeds and stems
-removed, chopped
OR
4 lg Yellow wax hot chiles
OR
4 ea Jalapenos
1/2 c  Water
Salt to taste
Combine the tomatoes, onion, and cilantro in a bowl.  Place the
chiles, water, and 3 tablespoons of the tomato, onion, cilantro
mixture in a blender or food processor and puree until smooth.  Add
the chile puree to the remaining tomato mixture and mix well.
Salt to taste.

Variations: Ecuadorians are very fond of adding beans to their salsa.
The most popular beans are the lupini, which are large white beans
about the size of Lima beans.  Just add the cooked beans directly to
the salsa. Or, add 1 teaspoon peanut butter with the chiles in the
blender and follow the above recipe.

The Authors' heat scale: Medium
