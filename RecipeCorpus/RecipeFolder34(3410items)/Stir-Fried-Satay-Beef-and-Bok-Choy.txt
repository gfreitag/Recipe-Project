Title: Stir-Fried Satay Beef and Bok Choy
Categories: Meats, Chinese, Wok, Vegetables
Yield: 4 servings
Ginger
1 lg Shallot; sliced thin
Salt to taste
1 lb Flank steak; cut into thin
Slices about 2-inches long
2 tb Vegetable oil
5 sl 1/4" thick peeled fresh
MMMMM--------------------------MARINADE-------------------------------
1 tb Soy sauce
1 tb Vegetable oil
2 tb Oyster sauce
1 tb Cornstarch
1 ts Sugar
2 tb Water
MMMMM-------------------------VEGETABLES------------------------------
1/2 ts Salt
1/2 ts Sugar
1 tb Water
1 tb Vegetable oil
1 lb Bok choy;trimmed and cut
Into 3-inch long pieces
1 lg Clove of garlic;chopped fine    1 1/2 ts Sesame oil
MMMMM---------------------------SAUCE--------------------------------
1 ts Cornstarch
2 ts Satay sauce
1/4 c  Water
FOR MARINADE: In medium bowl, combine all marinade ingredients. Add
beef and stir to coat with mixture; marinate for about 10 minutes.
FOR VEGETABLES: Heat wok over medium-high heat; add 1 tb vegetable
oil. Add bok choy, garlic, salt and sugar, stir-fry for 1 minute. Add
1 tb water and stir briefly; cover and cook for 2 minutes or until
bok choy is just tender. Add sesame oil and toss to mix; remove to
serving platter and keep warm.
FOR SAUCE: In small bowl, combine all sauce ingredients; set aside.
Heat 2 tb vegetable oil in wok over high heat. Add ginger and
shallot; stir-fry for 15 seconds. Add beef and stir-fry for 2
minutes. Add sauce mixture and stir-fry for about 1 minute or until
sauce has thickened. Taste and adjust seasonings with salt if
desired. Pour evenly over vegetables and serve immediately.
Source: Vancouver Sun  March 15/95
