Title: Pecan Pesto
Categories: Salsa/sauce, Nuts/grains
Yield: 1 batch
1/2 c  Vegetable oil
1 c  Grated Parmesan cheese
1/4 c  Grated Romano cheese
Salt and pepper; to taste
2 c  Fresh basil leaves
-- washed and dried
6    Garlic cloves
1 c  Shelled pecans
1/2 c  Olive oil mixed with
Combine basil, garlic and pecans in the bowl of a food processor; chop
coarsely.  With the motor still running, add the combined oils in a
slow, steady stream.  Shut the motor off and add the cheeses and salt
and pepper. Process briefly to combine.  Scrape the pesto into a bowl
and cover until ready to use.  This pesto can be stored in the
freezer, or refrigerated with a thin film of oil on top in a tightly
covered jar.

Yield: Approximately 2 1/2 cups.

From "Cooking with Herbs" by Emelie Tolley and Chris Mead.  New York:
Clarkson N. Potter, Inc., 1989.  Pg. 69.  Posted by Cathy Harned.
