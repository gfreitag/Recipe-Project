Title: Summer Fruit Compote with Mint and Cinnamon
Categories: Appetizer, Fruit, Side dish
Yield: 12 servings
1 lb Nectarines; peeled
1 lb Apricots; peeled
1 lb Red plums; peeled
1 lb Fresh black currants or
1 pt Berries or a mixture of both
Fresh mint; for garnish
2 1/2 c  ;Water
2/3 c  Plum jam
2    Sprigs fresh mint
1    Cinnamon stick
1 1/2 tb Raw sugar
1    Lemon; quartered
1 lb Peaches; peeled
In a large saucepan, combine the water, jam, mint, cinnamon, sugar and
lemon.  Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes.
Discard the mint and cinnamon.

Add the fruit in the order listed, simmering for 5 minutes between
each addition.  Continue until all the fruit except the currants or
berries is lightly cooked, soft but still firm.  Add the currants or
berries last; they require no cooking.

Remove the fruit from the heat, cool and chill.  Garnish with mint
before serving.

From Caroline Collis of Mott's Hall/England in "Cooking with Herbs" by
Emelie Tolley and Chris Mead.  New York: Clarkson N. Potter, Inc.,
1989. Pg. 199.  Posted by Cathy Harned.
