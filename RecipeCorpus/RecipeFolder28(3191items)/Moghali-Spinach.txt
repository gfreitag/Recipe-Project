Title: Moghali Spinach
Categories: Side dish, Indian, Vegetables
Yield: 6 servings
3 md Onions, cut into fine rings
1 ts Salt
1/4 ts Cayenne
1/2 ts Garam masala
3 lb Fresh spinach
1 ea 2" piece ginger
4 tb Vegetable oil
1/2 ts Whole fennel seeds
4 ea Whole cardamom pods
Wash spinach well & set aside.  Peel ginger & cut it into very fine
slices & then julienne.

Over a medium-high heat, heat oil in a skillet large enough to hold
all the spinach.  When hot, add fennel seeds & cardamom pods.  Stir
once & add onions & ginger.  Stir & fry until the onions turn a rich,
brown colour. Put in all the spinach, cover & aloow to wilt, stirring
every now & then. When wilted, reduce heat to medium, add salt &
cayenne & cook, covered, for 25 minutes.

Remove lid, add garam masala, stir & cook, uncovered, for another 5
minutes.  There should be hardly any liquid left in the skillet.

Madhur Jaffrey, "A Taste of India"
