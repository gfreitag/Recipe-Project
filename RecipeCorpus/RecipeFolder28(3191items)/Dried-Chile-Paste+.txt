Title: Dried Chile Paste+
Categories: Can/preserv, Chilies
Yield: 4 ounces
Boiling water
3 tb Vegatable oil
5    Dried chiles (ancho, pasilla
-or mulato) deveined and
-seeded
This could be for sauces, stews. soups, grilled cheese sandwiches,
pizza, or crisp crackers and cream cheese.

Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Lightly toast the chiles in the oven 3
to 5 minutes, or until they've puffed up. Place in a small bowl and
add just enough boiling water to cover. Let soak 20 minutes. Drain.
Place in a blender or food processor fitted with the steel blade and
puree with the oil to make a smooth paste. Store in a covered
container in the refrigerator. This will keep several months.
