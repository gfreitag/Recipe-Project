Title: Turkey-Vegetable Hot Pot (pressure cooker recipe)+
Categories: Poultry, Vegetables, Main dish
Yield: 2 trky drums
1    Bay leaf
2    Strips orange zest
1/2 ts Salt or to taste
2    Young turkey drumsticks
-(about 1 to 1 1/4 lb.) or
-2 thighs, skinned
10 oz Fresh or frozen (defrosted)
-green beans
1 tb Olive oil
1    Garlic clove, minced
1 lg Can tomatoes, coarsely
-chopped with the juice
1 1/2 lb New potatoes, (about 8)
-scrubbed
2 lg Carrots, cut in 4 or 5
-chunks
10 oz (about 8) small white onions    1 1/2 c  Fresh or frozen (thawed)
-corn kernals
1/3 c  Parsely or cilantro, finely
-minced
Salt to taste
Freshly ground black pepper
-to taste
-peeled
3    Ribs celery, finely chopped
1/3 c  Dried mushrooms, loosely
-packed (1/2 oz.)
1 ts Dried oregano
1/2 ts Dried Rosemary leaves or
-scant 1/4 ts. ground
Heat the olive oil in the cooker and saute the garlic for 10 seconds.
Add the tomatoes, potatoes, carrots, onions, celery, mushrooms,
oregano, rosemary, bay leaf, orange zest and salt. Stand the
drumsticks, meaty side down, in the soup.

Lock the lid and place over high heat; bring to pressure. Adjust the
heat to maintain high pressure and cook for 12 minutes. let the
pressure drop naturally or use a quick release method. Remove the
lid, tilting it away from you to allow any excess steam to escape.

Remove the drumsticks and cut the meat from the bone. Cut the meat in
1 inch chunks and return them to the pot. Discard the bones (or
reserve them for stock.) Stir in the green beans, corn and parsley
and cook over medium heat until the vegetables are cooked, about 3 to
5 minutes. Remove the orange zest and bay leaf and add salt and
pepper to taste.
