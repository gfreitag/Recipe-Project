England, 1381
Title: Apple Fritters
Categories: English, Medieval, Desserts, Fruit, Breads
Yield: 2 servings
2 ea To 3 apples
Confectioner's sugar; for
-garnish
3/4 c  Flour
4 ea Eggs; beaten
1 pn Pepper
1/8 ts Saffron
"Sift the flour and mix with the beaten eggs to make a smooth
batter. Add the pepper and saffron.
"Peel, core, and slice the apples, put them into the batter and
leave for 1 hour. This will allow the saffron to colour and flavour
the batter. Drop by large spoonfuls into deep fat heated to 375f and
fry for 3 to 4 minutes; the fritters will rise to the surface when
cooked and should be a golden brown. Drain, sprinkle with con-
fectioner's sugar and serve hot."

From _Ancient Cookery_, 1381 "For to make fritters: Nym flour and
eyerin and grind pepper and saffron and make thereto a batter & pare
apples and cut them to broad pieces and cast them therein and fry
them in the batter with fresh grease and serve it forth."
