Title: Chicken Chow Mein
Categories: Main dish, Healthy
Yield: 4 servings
6 oz Chicken breasts without
-- skin, boneless
-- coarsely chopped
2    Green onions; sliced
2 c  Bok choy; thinly sliced
1 1/2 c  Mixed vegetables, frozen
-- thawed and drained
1 cn Waterchestnuts, sliced
-- drained and rinsed
1 c  Bean sprouts, fresh
6 oz Chinese noodles, fresh egg
-- uncooked
Non-stick cooking spray
1/2 c  Chicken broth, 1/3
-- less-salt
2 tb Soy sauce, low sodium
1 1/2 ts Cornstarch
1/2 ts Sesame oil
1/2 ts Black pepper; ground
1/8 ts Chinese five-spice powder
Recipe by: Cooking Healthy Series Vol 1. # 5

1. Preheat oven to 400 F. Cook noodles according to package
directions, omitting salt. Drain and rinse well under cold water
until pasta is cool; drain well. Lightly spray 9-inch cake pan with
non-stick cooking spray. Spread noodles in pan, pressing firmly.
Lightly spray top of noodles with non-stick cooking spray. Bake 10
mins.

2. Invert moodles into baking sheet or large plate. Carefully slide
noodle cake back into cake pan. Bake 10 to 15 mins or until top is
crisp and lightly browned. Transfer to serving platter. Whisk
together next 6 ingredients in small bowl until cornstarch is
dissolved; set aside.

3. Spray large non-stick skillet with cooking spray. Add chicken and
green onions. Cook, stirring frequently, until chicken is no longer
pink, about 5 mins. Stir in bok choy, mixed vegetables, and water
chestnuts. Cook 3 mins or until vegetables are crisp-tender. Push
vegetables to one side of skillet; stir in sauce. Cook and stir until
thickened, about 2 mins. Stir in bean sprouts. Spoon over noodle cake.

Makes 4 servings
