Title: Stewed Chicken Feet With A Murshroom & Spring Onion Sauce
Categories: Poultry, Vietnamese
Yield: 4 servings
2 oz Dried Chinese mushrooms
Salt
2/3 ts Sugar
Vegetable oil -- for deep
Frying
12    Chicken feet, scrubbed,
Claws trimmed off
2 tb Dark soy sauce
3 sl Ginger root -- peeled
2    Green onions
2    Cloves garlic
Parsley -- for garnish
MMMMM-------------------------SEASONING------------------------------
1 tb White wine
1/2 c  Mushroom water
1 ts Light soy sauce
1 ts Oyster sauce
1 ts Nuoc Mam sauce -- optional
1 ts Sugar
1/4 ts Black pepper
Salt
1 ts Cornstarch mixed with a
Little water
* (Chan Ga Hap An Voi Xot Nam Hanh La).

1.  Wash the dried Chinese mushrooms and soak them for 2 hours.
Squeeze out the excess water and retain as stock.  Marinate for 1/2
hour with salt, sugar, and 1 tablespoon oil.  Steam for 12 minutes.
2. Blanch the chicken feet in boiling water for 5 minutes.  Remove
and coat with dark soy sauce. Deep-fry in hot oil until golden.
Drain, leaving 2 tablespoons oil for sauteing. 3. Grind the ginger,
green onions, and garlic. Heat the reserved oil in a casserole and
saute the ginger, gree nonion, and garlic until fragrant. Add the
chicken feet and stir-fry. 4. In a casserole sizzle the wine, add the
mushroom water and seasoning except for the cornstarch and stew for
an hour.  Mix in the mushrooms and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove the
lid and thicken the sauce with the cornstarch. Serve hot in the
casserol
