Title: Oldtime Oyster Dressing
Categories: Side dish, Sthrn/livng, Dressing, Clam/oyster
Yield: 1 unknown
-minced
1/2 c  Butter; or melted turkey fat
1/2 ts Pepper
Salt; to taste
1/2 ts Sage; or more if desired
2    Eggs; raw
Stock
24    Oysters; opt.
10 c  Breadcrumbs, stale dry;
-(mostly cornbread mixed
-with a few stale biscuits
-and toast)
Water; cold, or stock to
-moisten
1 lg Onion; finely minced
1 c  Celery; pre-cooked, finely
Sprinkle crumbs with cold water or stock and let stand 30 minutes to
1 hour to fluff.  Saute onion and celery in butter.  Combine all
ingredients, addint stock last and using just enough to moisten
sufficiently so that a few crumbs pressed between fingers will hold
together. Stuff lightly into turkey.

To make oyster stuffing, add 2 dozen oysters with a little of their
liquid to the above recipe.

Yield: enough for a 10 to 12 pound turkey.

SOURCE: Southern Living Magazine, November 1972. Typos by Nancy
