Title: Steamed Cranberry Pudding
Categories: Dessert, Fruit, Holiday, Pudding
Yield: 6 - 8 folks
MMMMM--------------------------PUDDING-------------------------------
1 1/2 c  Sifted flour;
1/2 c  Light molasses;
2 ts Baking soda;
1/4 ts Salt;
2 c  Cranberries;
1 c  Raisins;
1/2 c  Nuts; chopped
1/2 c  Dates; chopped
MMMMM-------------------------HARD SAUCE------------------------------
1 1/2 c  Confectioners' sugar; sifted
2 tb Light rum;
1/4 c  Butter -=OR=-
1/4 c  Margarine; softened
FOR PUDDING: Mix berries, raisins, nuts and dates.  Add flour and
stir. Combine molasses, soda, salt and 1/3 cup boiling water.  Stir
into fruit mixture. Pour into a 6-cup mold.  Cover tightly with foil
and tie with string. Place on a rack in a deep kettle.  Add boiling
water 1" deep. Cover kettle.  Add boiling water 1" deep.  Cover
kettle and steam 1-1/4 hours, adding water if needed.  Cool 10
minutes and then unmold. Serve warm with hard sauce. FOR HARD SAUCE:
In a medium bowl, heat butter at high speed until light. Add sugar.
Add sugar gradually, beat until sauce is smooth and fluffy. Beat in
run. Source: The San Diego Union-Tribune Food Section, Nov. 10, 1994
and "The Cranberry Connection" by Beatrice Ross Buszek. Brought to
you and yours via Nancy O'Brion and her Meal-Master. Submitted By
