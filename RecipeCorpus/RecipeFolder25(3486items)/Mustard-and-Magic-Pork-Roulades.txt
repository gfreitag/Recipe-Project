Title: Mustard and Magic Pork Roulades
Categories: Pork, Main dish, Herbs/spice
Yield: 18 servings
MMMMM----------------SOURCE: THE NATIONAL CULINAR---------------------
2 T  Grenadine
1/4 c  Dried currants
1/4 c  Dried apricots, diced
1 T  Orange zest, chopped fine
1 t  Fresh thyme, chopped
1 1/2 c  Ritz cracker crumbs
1 T  Fresh rosemary, chopped
2/3 c  Grey Poupon Dijon mustard
2 c  Brown sauce (Espagnole)
3/4 c  Grey Poupon Dijon mustard
2/3 c  Currant jelly
1 c  Sour cream
Second Prize 1991-1992
-Randall Colman, CEC, CCE
-Sullivan County
Culinary Association.
6 ea Pork tenderloin, trimmed
20 ea Ounces onions, sliced
--1/4-inch thick
1 ea Ounce butter, clarified
Salt and white pepper to
-taste
3 T  Sugar
3 T  Red wine vinegar
2 ea Ounces red wine
MMMMM----------------METHOD: BUTTERFLY TENDERLOIN---------------------
Mixture on tenderloins,
-leaving a 1-inch border.
-Roll tenderloins
And secure with butcher's
-twine.  Combine cracker
-crumbs and
Rosemary. Brush tenderloins
-with 2/3 cup Dijon mustard
-and coat
With crumb mixture.  Roast
-tenderloins in preheated
-325' oven until
An internal temperature of
-150' is reached.
Season with salt and
-pepper.  Sweat onions in
-clarified butter for 5
Minutes; do not brown.  Add
-salt, pepper and sugar and
-continue to
Sweat; do not brown.  Add
-vinegar, wine, grenadine,
-currants,
Apricots and orange zest.
-Simmer uncovered for 30
-minutes or until
A marmalade consistency is
-formed.  Add thyme and
-cool.  Spread
To make sauce: Combine brown sauce, 3/4 cup Dijon mustard and currant
jelly and bring to a simmer; temper in sour cream.  Remove
tenderloins from oven and allow to stand for 5 minutes.  Place 2
ounces of sauce on each plate. Remove twine and slice tenderloins
into medallions, fanning out portions on top of sauce.
