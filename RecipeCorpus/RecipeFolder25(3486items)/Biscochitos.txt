Title: Biscochitos
Categories: Holiday, Cake/cookie, Tex/mexican
Yield: 1 batch
2 ts Anise seed
2    Eggs
1/4 c  Brandy
1/4 c  Sugar
1 tb Cinnamon
6 c  Flour
1/4 ts Salt
3 ts Baking powder
1 lb Lard
-- or substitute shortening
1 1/2 c  Sugar
Sift flour with baking powder and salt.  Cream lard with sugar and
anise seed until fluffy.  Beat in eggs one at a time.  Mix in flour
and brandy until well blended.  Turn dough out on a floured board and
pat or roll to 1/4" to 1/2" thick.  Cut into shapes (the fleur-de-lis
is traditional). Dust with a mixture of sugar and cinnamon.  Bake 10
minutes at 350 F. or until lightly browned.

Carrejo writes: "This is New Mexico's traditional cookie, baked
mostly at Christmas."

From Paulita T.Carrejo/Apache Creek, NM in "The Kitchen Table: Where
Herbs and Spices Make a Difference" column in "The Herb Companion."
