Title: Gilroy Garlic Festival's Garlic Jelly
Categories: Can/preserv, Herbs/spice
Yield: 1 batch
-or-
2 pk Powdered pectin
-- (1 3/4 to 2 oz. each)
Food coloring (opt'l.)
1/2 c  Fine-chopped garlic
3 c  White wine vinegar (approx.)
2 c  ;Water
6 c  Sugar
2    3 oz. pouches liquid pectin
In a 2 to 2 1/2 quart pan, combine garlic and vinegar.  Simmer gently,
uncovered, over medium heat for 15 minutes.  Remove from heat and
pour into a 1 quart glass jar.  Cover and let stand at room
temperature for 24 to 36 hours.

Pour flavored vinegar through a wire strainer into a bowl, pressing
garlic with the back of a spoon to squeeze out as much liquid as
possible. Discard residue.  Measure liquid and add vinegar if needed
to make 2 cups.

To use liquid pectin: In a 5 to 6-quart kettle, combine flavored
vinegar, water and sugar.  Bring to a full, rolling boil over
medium-high heat. Stir in pectin and bring to a boil that cannot be
stirred down. Boil, stirring constantly, for 1 minute.

To use dry pectin: In a 5 to 6-quart kettle, combine flavored vinegar,
water and pectin.  Bring to a full, rolling boil over medium-high
heat, then stir in the sugar.  Stirring constantly, bring to a boil
that cannot be stirred down and boil for 2 minutes.

If desired, stir in 2 drops of red, yellow or orange food coloring.
Skim off and discard foam, then spoon hot jelly into hot, sterilized,
1/2-pint canning jars to within 1/4" of rim.  Wipe rims clean with a
damp cloth; top with scalded lids and bands.

Place jars on a rack in a canning kettle and cover with boiling water.
Simmer for 10 minutes.  Lift jars from canner and set on folded
towels to cool.  Before storing, test for a good seal; refrigerate
any jars that did not seal.

Yield: 3 1/2 pints.
